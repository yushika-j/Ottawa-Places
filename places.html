Featured Places

Here you can find information about the best places to visit in Ottawa.

Parliament Hill

Parliament Hill is a must-see for any visitor to Ottawa. The seat of Canada's federal government, this grand neo-Gothic complex is set on a lovely hill above the Ottawa River. The Changing of the Guard ceremony takes place on the front lawn of Parliament Hill each morning in the summer months.

National Gallery of Canada

The National Gallery of Canada is one of the country's premier art galleries and home to some of the world's most extensive collections of Canadian art. The gallery's glass and granite structure is an Ottawa landmark, and inside, you'll find works of art from Canadian artists like the Group of Seven and Emily Carr.

Rideau Canal

The Rideau Canal is a beautiful waterway that connects Ottawa to Lake Ontario and the Saint Lawrence River. In the summer, the canal is a great place for boating, cycling, and walking, and in the winter, it transforms into the world's largest skating rink.

Canadian Museum of History

The Canadian Museum of History offers a fascinating journey through Canada's rich history and cultural heritage. From indigenous artifacts to interactive exhibits, visitors can explore the diverse stories that have shaped the nation.

ByWard Market

ByWard Market is one of Canada's oldest and largest public markets, offering an array of fresh produce, artisanal crafts, and international cuisine. Stroll through the bustling streets, enjoy street performances, and sample local delights.

Canadian War Museum

The Canadian War Museum is dedicated to preserving and showcasing Canada's military history. With interactive displays, historic artifacts, and immersive exhibitions, visitors can gain insight into the country's wartime experiences.

Gatineau Park

Gatineau Park offers a serene escape from the city with its lush forests, scenic trails, and picturesque lakes. Outdoor enthusiasts can enjoy hiking, biking, picnicking, and wildlife spotting amidst breathtaking natural beauty.

Canadian Museum of Nature

The Canadian Museum of Nature is home to extensive collections of natural history specimens, including fossils, minerals, and live animals. Explore exhibits on Canada's diverse ecosystems, evolution, and conservation efforts.

Lansdowne Park

Lansdowne Park is a vibrant urban destination featuring shops, restaurants, entertainment venues, and green spaces. Attend a sporting event, catch a live performance, or simply relax and soak in the lively atmosphere.
